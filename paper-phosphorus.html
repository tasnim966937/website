The Research

This study breaks new ground by demonstrating that Google's Satellite Embeddings, a compact 64-dimensional representation of multi-sensor Earth observation data, can accurately predict three critical crop biochemical parameters: canopy chlorophyll (CCC), nitrogen (CNC), and phosphorus (CPC) content.

We tested 11 machine learning regression algorithms across four dimensionality-reduction workflows (FBSR, PBSL, CIRBS, and VOPSS), systematically sweeping from 1 to 64 features. Regularized linear models (Ridge, ElasticNet, Lasso) consistently delivered the most compact, stable predictions with the narrowest AOI distributions. This is the first study to successfully retrieve canopy phosphorus from any satellite-derived embedding product.
