The Research

This study explores the potential of EnMAP hyperspectral satellite imagery for retrieving canopy chlorophyll content (CCC) and canopy nitrogen content (CNC) in corn fields. We compared empirical machine learning approaches with hybrid PROSAIL radiative transfer models, testing six MLRAs with band analysis tools to find the most informative spectral features.

A key contribution is the discovery that the traditional nitrogen conversion factor of 4.43 does not work well for corn. We established a new conversion factor of 3.03, which significantly improved the alignment between simulated and field-measured CNC values. Both KRR and GPR emerged as top performers, delivering R² values of 0.93 for CCC and 0.94 for CNC.
